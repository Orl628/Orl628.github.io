The chain event graph of a labeled tree

February 19, 2021

When studying chain event graphs, it was not clear to me how these objects are defined. Although their construction from staged trees seemed intuitive, I could not find a truly formal definition in the literature. Below is my attempt at such a definition.

Let \(T\) be a rooted tree and \(L\) an arbitrary label set. A labeling of \(T\) is a map \(\theta\colon E(T) \to L\). We call the pair \((T, \theta)\) a labeled tree.

Every labeled tree has a natural staging induced by the edge labels. Conversely, every staged tree has the canonical labeling \(\theta\colon E(T) \to \bigcup_{f \text{ floret}} \bigcup_{e\in f}\{e\}\) with \(\theta(e) = e\).

Let \((T, \theta)\) be a labeled tree. A labeled subtree \((T', \theta')\) of \(T\) is a labeled tree such that \(V(T')\subseteq V(T)\), \(E(T')\subseteq E(T )\) and such that for every edge \(e = (v, w)\) of \(T\), if \(v \in V(T')\) then \(w \in V(T')\), \(e \in E(T')\) and \(\theta'(e) = \theta(e)\).

By this definition, a labeled subtree is already determined by the choice of its root. However, different choices of the root might lead to the same labeled subtree. Also, two vertices of \(T\) are in the same position if and only if their induced labeled subtrees are the same.

Given a labeled tree \((T, \theta)\), we define its labeled chain event graph \((G, \vartheta)\) as follows.

1. Set \(V(G) = \{\text{Labeled subtrees of } T\}\).

2. For \(T_1\), \(T_2 \in V(G)\), define \(E(T_1 , T_2 )\) to be the set of edges \(e = (r_1 , r_2 ) \in E(T_1 )\) such that \(r_1\) is the root of \(T_1\) and \(r_2\) is the root of a copy of \(T_2\) appearing as a labeled subtree of \(T_1\).

3. For \(e \in E(T_1 , T_2 )\), let its label \(\vartheta(e)\) be the label of \(e\) in \(T_1\).

This concludes my definition of the chain event graph associated to a given staged tree.
