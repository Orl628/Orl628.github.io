Orlando Marigliano

Algebraic statistics, applied algebraic geometry at MPI Leipzig

Research

My background is in algebraic geometry. I am interested in the connections between algebraic geometry and the fields of statistics, data science, and the natural sciences. I am a Ph.D. student co-advised by Bernd Sturmfels and Christiane Görgen.

Topics currently on my mind: Linear Gaussian covariance models, complete quartics, multivariate boundary polynomial regression, Bayesian networks, staged trees, the maximum likelihood degree, models with ML degree one.

Other topics I'm interested in: random geometry and topology, singular learning theory, loss landscapes.
